## Purpose
This notice catalogues all external license terms that apply to components bundled with or referenced by the project. Its purpose is to help users, reviewers, and distributors understand that some parts of the application remain governed by separate legal instruments.

## Scope
It applies to binary dependencies, icons, fonts, test libraries, SDKs, and any redistributable materials that are not authored solely by the project team. It should also cover services that impose contractual terms on the use of APIs, analytics, advertising, or cloud endpoints.

## Operational Commitments
Each third-party item should be listed with its license name, source, and notable obligations such as attribution, redistribution limits, patent clauses, or copyleft duties. The notice should avoid vague catch-all language that makes compliance impossible to verify later.

## Application to Master QR
Master QR’s dependency footprint should be matched against the shipped release artifact, not merely against the source tree. This matters because disabled, commented, or optional libraries can still become active in a later build if product strategy changes.

## Review and Governance
The notice should be reviewed whenever a new SDK, font, icon set, or build-time plugin is introduced. A repository-level license audit is recommended so the public notice always reflects the bundle actually delivered to end users.
