## Purpose
This plan sets out how the organization will detect, triage, investigate, contain, remediate, and communicate about security incidents, including those that may involve unauthorized access to personal data. It converts abstract security intent into an executable operating procedure.

## Scope
It applies to compromised accounts, malicious code, lost devices, log exposure, improper disclosures, and any confirmed or suspected breach affecting the app or its supporting systems. The plan should assign roles, escalation paths, decision deadlines, and evidence preservation duties.

## Operational Commitments
Good response planning requires speed without recklessness. The team should know how to preserve forensic evidence, limit further exposure, notify affected parties when required, and capture a post-incident learning record that feeds future hardening work.

## Application to Master QR
Master QR’s incident plan should pay attention to analytics dashboards, ad SDK integrations, release signing keys, and any local or remote data stores that could reveal user behavior. Even a lightweight app can suffer a damaging incident if privacy assumptions are broken.

## Review and Governance
The plan should be tested periodically and after every significant architecture change. Post-incident reviews should generate concrete corrective actions and deadlines, not just descriptive summaries.
