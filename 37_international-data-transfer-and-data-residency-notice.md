## Purpose
This notice explains when data may move across borders, which jurisdictions may receive it, and how residency commitments are handled. It is meant to help users understand that cloud-based services and third-party SDKs may process information outside the user’s home country.

## Scope
It applies to analytics, ads, support systems, backups, remote storage, and any hosted service used by the application or its operators. The notice should identify whether transfers are occasional, continuous, or restricted to specific vendors and should distinguish storage location from access location where relevant.

## Operational Commitments
If the product promises residency in a particular region, that promise must be exact and enforceable. If it does not, the notice should avoid implying local-only processing where external services are actually involved. The language should also mention safeguards such as contractual protections or minimization where applicable.

## Application to Master QR
Master QR appears to be globally distributed and therefore may rely on third-party services that process data in multiple regions. The notice should make that reality visible while also clarifying that the core QR scanning function can remain local to the device.

## Review and Governance
The notice should be reviewed whenever the vendor stack changes or a new region is added. Any residency claim must be matched by infrastructure and contract evidence.
