# Accessibility and Usability Guidelines

The application should be usable by a wide audience, including users with limited dexterity, low vision, or non-default interaction preferences. Accessibility is not only a design concern but also a legal and market-compliance concern because legal notices, permission prompts, and consent dialogs must be understandable and navigable. Master QR should keep touch targets sufficiently large, maintain visible contrast, and avoid interaction patterns that depend solely on color or motion. Critical disclosures such as camera consent and ad personalization should be reachable by assistive technologies and readable without excessive scrolling. If the product adds richer content later, it should continue to treat accessibility as a release criterion rather than an optional enhancement.
