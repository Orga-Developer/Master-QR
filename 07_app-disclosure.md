## Purpose
This disclosure explains what the application does, what data it touches, and which external services may be involved in its operation. The document is designed to be short enough for store review, but precise enough to prevent misunderstanding about the app’s core functionality.

## Scope
It applies to app-store listings, in-app help pages, consent screens, and support articles. The disclosure should not be treated as a promotional text; it is a factual statement of product behavior, including scanning, generation, history, sharing, and any ancillary network calls.

## Operational Commitments
All disclosures must distinguish between on-device processing and server-dependent features. If the app processes a QR code locally, the disclosure should say so plainly. If it uses advertising, analytics, or consent tooling, those dependencies must be disclosed in ordinary language that users can understand.

## Application to Master QR
Master QR is primarily a QR scanner and generator with local history, multilingual UI, and ad/analytics integration points. The disclosure should clearly state that users may scan, create, save, and share codes, and that some features may involve external SDKs even when the core QR workflow remains local.

## Review and Governance
The disclosure should be refreshed whenever the feature set changes, especially if accounts, cloud sync, biometrics, or payment features are added in a later version.
