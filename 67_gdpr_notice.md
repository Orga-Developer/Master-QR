# GDPR Notice

This document sets out the governing standard for GDPR Notice within the legal architecture of Master QR. Master QR is a camera-first Android application that scans QR codes, generates QR codes, stores scan history locally in Room, supports language switching, provides share/export flows, integrates advertising and analytics components, and is distributed across mobile app stores with platform-specific compliance requirements. The document is written in advanced legal English so it can serve as a publication-ready internal policy, a store-review support artifact, and a future contract annex. It is intentionally precise about scope, permissions, data categories, and release governance, so the text can survive scrutiny across multiple jurisdictions and app ecosystems.

Applicability to Master QR: This notice establishes the GDPR-facing framework for users in the EEA and related regions. It should describe lawful bases for processing, data minimization, retention, recipients, international transfer safeguards, and the user’s rights to access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, and portability. For Master QR, the document should also distinguish between strictly necessary processing for app operation and optional processing tied to analytics or ads. Because the app’s main functionality can be delivered with little or no account data, the notice should emphasize privacy by default and purpose limitation. The policy must therefore distinguish between what the application actually does today and what it merely might do in a future roadmap. In the current codebase, that means recognizing camera-based scanning, QR generation, local history management, language preferences, optional exporting, advertising or analytics integrations, and the absence of broad account-based processing.

Operational requirements: the developer should document purpose limitation, legal basis where relevant, retention limits, access restrictions, vendor controls, user notice requirements, and escalation paths for exceptions. If a feature is not present, the document should not pretend that it is; instead it should state that the control is reserved for future expansion. That approach is especially valuable for store review, compliance review, and internal audit preparation.
