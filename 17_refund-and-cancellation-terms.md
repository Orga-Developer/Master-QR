## Purpose
These terms define how purchases, renewals, cancellations, and refund requests are handled where the application or its related services involve paid features. The document should clearly separate app-store payment mechanics from any direct merchant relationship the operator may or may not maintain.

## Scope
It applies to subscriptions, one-time purchases, promotional trials, and any premium digital goods that are offered now or in the future. The terms should identify who processes the payment, how cancellation is initiated, and what happens to access after a refund or billing dispute.

## Operational Commitments
Refund expectations should not be exaggerated. The policy should explain that store rules, consumer protection law, and payment-provider procedures may control the ultimate outcome. Where the app has no paid feature today, the document can still state that any future paid tier will be governed by the then-current store and consumer rules.

## Application to Master QR
For Master QR, this document is mainly conditional unless a premium layer is later added. Its inclusion is still valuable because it establishes a clean legal framework before the commercial model expands beyond free, ad-supported access.

## Review and Governance
The terms should be updated before any paid SKU goes live. Payment copy, regional tax language, and recurring billing disclosures should be validated against the live store configuration.
