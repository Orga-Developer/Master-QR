# User Rights and Data Subject Access Request Procedure

**Product:** Master QR  
**Package:** com.qrcodereader.app  
**Effective Date:** [INSERT DATE]  
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose
This policy and procedure describe how users may exercise rights over their data, including access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, and portability where those rights are available under applicable law. It also sets a practical process for verifying, tracking, and completing requests.

## Scope
It applies to privacy requests, legal inquiries, support tickets, and any workflow that touches personal data. The procedure should explain what proof may be needed, how identity is verified, expected response times, and whether the request will be fulfilled in-app, by email, or through a web form.

## Operational Commitments
A good rights process is transparent and predictable. Users should not be forced to navigate multiple departments or repeat the same request in several channels. The operator should also preserve a record of the request and outcome for compliance audit purposes, while minimizing any extra data stored beyond what is necessary.

## Application to Master QR
For Master QR, a rights process should cover local history data, consent artifacts, SDK-derived identifiers where applicable, and any support records linked to the app. Even if the app remains mostly local, users should still have a clear path to ask questions or request deletion of information held externally.

## Review and Governance
The procedure should be reviewed whenever legal bases, countries of distribution, or account features change. Staff should receive a standard response template to reduce delay and inconsistency.
