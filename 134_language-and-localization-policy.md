# Language and Localization Policy

The application’s multilingual behavior must be governed by an explicit localization policy because consent notices, privacy disclosures, and support instructions lose legal force when they are incomprehensible to the user. Master QR should ensure that each supported language is reviewed for meaning, not merely translated mechanically. Any legal page rendered in-app should preserve semantic parity across languages, especially where user rights, data retention, or advertising disclosures are discussed. The interface must avoid mixing untranslated fragments that could confuse consent or create accessibility barriers. When new locales are added, the application should also re-check date formats, numeric conventions, and cultural expectations so that disclosures remain legible and trustworthy in every supported market.
