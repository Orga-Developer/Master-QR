# QR Content Safety Policy

The application may decode QR payloads that contain URLs, text, contact data, or other machine-readable content. Because QR codes can be used for phishing, malware distribution, or deceptive redirection, Master QR must treat decoded content as untrusted until the user explicitly chooses an action. The app should avoid automatic navigation to links without a visible warning and should distinguish between text display and external execution. Security messaging should advise users that the application is a reader, not a guarantor of content authenticity. Any optional link-opening behavior should be accompanied by a human-readable preview, domain awareness, and a safe exit path. This policy is essential for store review, anti-abuse posture, and user trust maintenance.
